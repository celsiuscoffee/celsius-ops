About this manual

This is the single source of truth for the Pickup app's product surface. It maps every feature to a purpose, and every purpose to a metric — so we never ship a UI box we can't justify, and never claim a win we can't measure.

?

How to read this

Each screen entry has three parts: Purpose (the one-line reason it exists), Features (every meaningful UI box on the screen with its role), and Metrics (what we measure to know it's pulling its weight). Pills mark instrumentation state: tracked means an Amplitude event fires today; gap means the metric matters but isn't wired yet.

ii.

The product

The Pickup app is the Celsius Coffee customer's primary channel for ordering ahead, earning loyalty points, and skipping the queue. It runs as a native iOS/Android Expo app, bundled with OTA updates on the .pickup.next channel during development.

TechExpo Router · React Native · Supabase backend · Stripe PaymentSheet · Amplitude analytics · Expo Push (APNs + FCM V1)
Outlets supportedShah Alam, Conezion, Tamarind — selected per session before browsing
PaymentsCard, Apple Pay, FPX (Malaysia), GrabPay — Stripe-mediated. Zero-amount bypass for fully-discounted orders.
Loyalty modelPoints (Beans) · tiered membership · vouchers · missions · mystery drops · referrals · annual Wrapped
AuthPhone (Malaysian +60) → OTP, browse-without-auth fallback for menu/posters
iii.

App-level objectives

Every feature in this manual ladders up to one of six objectives. If a feature doesn't, it shouldn't exist.

↻

Drive repeat purchase

Coffee is habitual; LTV is the business. We want every customer ordering more often than they would without the app.

North-star orders / active member / month · D30 repeat rate
⏵

Skip the queue

The core promise vs. walk-in. If app orders aren't faster, the product fails.

North-star app share of outlet orders · median pickup wait
＋

Increase basket size

Modifiers, recommendations, and missions should lift the average order.

North-star AOV (RM) · items per order · modifier attach rate
✦

Build loyalty habit

Points, tiers, and vouchers exist to convert visits into a quarterly streak.

North-star active member ratio · voucher redemption %
∞

Cheap viral CAC

Referrals and Wrapped should beat paid acquisition cost-per-customer.

North-star referral → first-order conversion · CAC vs. paid
⚙

Operational efficiency

Less barista friction, fewer disputes, faster handoff at the counter.

North-star auto-collection rate · order dispute rate

!

The gap is wide

11 events cover roughly 15% of the app's surface. The funnel between login → home → menu → PDP → cart → checkout works, but nothing measures rewards, mystery, referral, wrapped, tier, or order-detail outcomes. Amplitude is also gated on a non-empty EXPO_PUBLIC_AMPLITUDE_API_KEY + a fresh native build — confirm both before treating any number as real.

If you fix five events, do these
  1. screen_viewed · the funnel-for-free move

    Fire on every screen mount with screen_name. Unlocks landing-rate metrics for every screen above without any per-screen wiring.

  2. voucher_claimed + voucher_used · the loyalty loop

    Issued ÷ claimed ÷ used. Without these we can't tell whether vouchers are a P&L expense or a growth lever.

  3. order_collected · closes the order funnel

    Include the method (swipe / auto / barista_marked). Measures the swipe-to-collect feature and tells us how often the queue is actually being skipped.

  4. reorder_tapped · the highest-value action

    One-tap reorder is the cheapest path to a repeat order. Track CTR and reorder → order_placed conversion.

  5. poster_tapped · proves marketing works

    Include poster_id + deeplink. Without this, promos are pure faith.
